Difficult information could be a list of dates, or a series of ten steps which don't logically follow each other. Difficult information may be abstract, arbitrary, or simply be from a subject that you don't have many models to draw from. In these cases, applying linking methods can ensure those ideas aren't forgotten.

Critical information is like the foundation of a building. It forms the base from which you construct many other ideas from. A beginner learning about matrices would find that determinants and row reduction are both extremely crucial topics. Failing to understand these ideas (even slightly) could ruin any possibility of learning hundreds of other ideas from which they are based.

Information that isn’t particularly crucial or difficult, doesn’t warrant special techniques. Since much of the learning process is subconscious, you will probably form enough associations and models for it to stick without the intervention of a special technique.
