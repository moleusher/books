For example, let's say you were in a psychology lecture learning about classical conditioning. Classical conditioning is the process of associating a stimulus and a response and was made famous by Pavlov's dogs. Pavlov noticed that by conditioning the dogs to associate a ringing bell with the arrival of food, the dogs would salivate when he rang a bell. After several times, the dogs would salivated when hearing a bell, even if he didn't bring food.

How could you use metaphor to learn this? The first step in metaphor is to look for something in your experience that models this process. As I grew up with Canadian winters, the first metaphor I imagined was winters, the first metaphor I imagined was walking through snow.

When you first walk through snow, every possible path is equal because they are all densely filled with powder. But after several walks through the snow, the first path you chose will become easier to walk through. This is because the compacting of snow under your feet creates a trail. Soon it is far easier to walk through one path than any other.
